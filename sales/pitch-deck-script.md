# ArgusReach — Sales Call Script & Pitch Deck Content

## Call Structure (15-minute strategy call)

### 0:00–1:30 — Opening (don't pitch yet)
"Hey [Name], thanks for making time. I know 15 minutes goes fast so I'll be direct — before I tell you anything about what we do, I want to make sure this is even relevant for you. Can I ask you a couple of quick questions?"

→ They say yes

"How are you currently getting new clients? And roughly, what percentage of your new business comes from referrals vs. anything proactive?"

*Listen. Most will say: 80–100% referrals. This is your opening.*

---

### 1:30–4:00 — Diagnose the Problem

**If they say 80%+ referrals:**
"Yeah, that's exactly where most [advisors/agencies/practices] are at. The referral model works — until it doesn't. The problem is it's not in your control. You have a great quarter, but if your top referral source retires, changes firms, or just gets busy — the pipeline dries up and you can't do anything about it."

"Have you ever tried building any kind of proactive outreach? Cold email, LinkedIn, anything?"

*Listen to their experience — did they try and fail? Never tried? Tried and gave up?*

**If they tried and it didn't work:**
"Yeah, generic outreach doesn't work anymore. People get 200 emails a day. What's changed in the last 2 years is that AI can now write outreach that actually references specific things about the person — their company, their role, something they're going through. It reads like a human wrote it, because it did — AI-assisted, not AI-generated."

---

### 4:00–7:00 — Introduce ArgusReach

"So what we do at ArgusReach — we're basically a done-for-you outbound system. We find your ideal prospects, build the list, write every email, run the sequences, handle the follow-up, and when someone replies positively, we route them to your calendar. You show up to the meeting. That's it."

"We work specifically with financial services and professional services firms in Florida — so we know how to write to CFOs, business owners, HR directors, surgeons. We're not a generic agency."

"The way we run outreach — it goes out as you. Your name, your firm. The prospect never knows we're involved. We're like a behind-the-scenes SDR team."

---

### 7:00–10:00 — Proof Points

"Let me give you a sense of what the numbers look like. With a properly targeted, personalized cold email sequence:"
- "Open rates are typically 35–55% — compared to the industry average of 15–20%."
- "Positive reply rate is 4–8% of contacts reached."
- "On our Starter plan — 200 contacts a month — that's 8–16 qualified conversations started. Not cold calls. Not ads. Real people who replied and expressed interest."

"For context: a junior SDR hire costs $60–70K a year before benefits. Our Starter plan is $750 a month."

---

### 10:00–12:30 — Handle Objections

**"I've tried cold email and it didn't work."**
"What were you sending? Generic template? Mail merge? That's the problem — it's not the channel, it's the execution. Spray-and-pray is dead. Personalized, one-at-a-time-style outreach at scale — that's what works."

**"I don't have time to manage another vendor."**
"You don't manage us. After one onboarding call — maybe 45 minutes — we build your targeting, write your sequences, and launch. Your only job is showing up to the meetings. We handle everything else."

**"How do I know it'll work for my business?"**
"Honestly, the only way to know is to run it. That's why we do a 3-month engagement — it takes 4–6 weeks to fully optimize targeting and sequences. The clients who get the most out of it let us iterate. We're not promising 20 meetings in month one. We're building you a consistent, predictable pipeline."

**"I have compliance concerns." (RIAs)**
"Fair — and that's something we address in onboarding. We don't make specific performance promises in the outreach. Every email is subject to your review before we launch. You approve the copy. We execute."

---

### 12:30–15:00 — Close

"Based on what you've told me, it sounds like this could be a fit. Here's what I'd suggest:"

"We do a proper 45-minute onboarding call where we build out your ICP, define your ideal prospect profile, and I show you exactly what the outreach will look like before a single email goes out. There's a $500 one-time setup fee to cover that work, and then we're on a 3-month engagement from there."

"The question is — do you want to keep relying entirely on referrals, or do you want a second engine running in the background that's filling your pipeline every single month?"

→ If yes: "Great. I'll send you a one-page agreement and an invoice for the setup fee. We can do the onboarding call as soon as next week."

→ If not ready: "No problem. Let me send you our one-pager so you have the details. What's a better time to follow up — end of this month, or early next?"

## Common Profile Notes

### RIA / Wealth Advisor
- Most sensitive to: compliance, reputation, client confidentiality
- Biggest fear: outreach sounds salesy or inappropriate
- Best approach: "Professional, direct, no promises — just an introduction"
- Decision speed: moderate (2–3 week close cycle)

### Insurance Agency Owner
- Most sensitive to: ROI, cost vs. hiring
- Biggest fear: outreach generates complaints
- Best approach: ROI math — cost of one new client vs. monthly fee
- Decision speed: fast (1 week close cycle)

### PT Practice Owner
- Most sensitive to: physician relationships being damaged
- Biggest fear: outreach comes across as cold or transactional
- Best approach: "We draft warm, collegial language — peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-doctor"
- Decision speed: moderate (depends on cash flow / growth stage)
